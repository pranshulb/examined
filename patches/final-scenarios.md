# Final Scenario Text Review - Findings

Reviewed all 24 scenario texts in index-v3.3.html for awkward sentences, typos, and punctuation issues.

## Issues Found & Fixed

### 1. **Scenario 0 (The Promise)** - Awkward phrasing
**Location:** Body text, line describing the sister  
**Issue:** "rebuilt her life around the absence of" is awkward and unclear  
**Original:** `something she has clearly moved on from, rebuilt her life around the absence of.`  
**Fixed to:** `something she has clearly moved on from, rebuilding her life in its absence.`

### 2. **Scenario 9 (The Last Question)** - Run-on sentence
**Location:** Body text, second sentence  
**Issue:** Run-on sentence with comma splice between independent clauses  
**Original:** `You have been making choices for the last several minutes that you already knew the answers to before you clicked, perhaps you have known them for years but never said them plainly enough to feel their weight.`  
**Fixed to:** `You have been making choices for the last several minutes that you already knew the answers to before you clicked; perhaps you have known them for years but never said them plainly enough to feel their weight.`

### 3. **Scenario 10 (The Library of Babel)** - Quote mark inconsistency
**Location:** Body text, dialogue about AI  
**Issue:** Inconsistent quotation marks within quotes  
**Original:** `they shrug and say "the AI told me."`  
**Fixed to:** `they shrug and say 'the AI told me.'`  
*(Changed inner quotes to single quotes for proper nesting)*

### 4. **Scenario 21 (The Banking Hours)** - British/American spelling inconsistency
**Location:** Body text, first sentence  
**Issue:** Uses British spelling "programme" when rest of document uses American English  
**Original:** `raise the pass rate by fifteen percent or the programme gets cut.`  
**Fixed to:** `raise the pass rate by fifteen percent or the program gets cut.`

## Minor Notes (No changes needed, but flagged for awareness)

- **Scenario 2 (The Dinner Party):** "the whole wretched dossier" is slightly dramatic but works well for the tone
- **Scenario 18 (The Whistleblower's Weight):** The triple "not X exactly" construction is intentional stylistic repetition that works

## Summary

**Total issues fixed:** 4  
**Severity:** Minor (clarity and consistency improvements)  
**Impact:** Improved readability and consistency throughout all scenario texts

All fixes maintain the philosophical tone and narrative voice while improving clarity and grammatical correctness.
