## THE PHILOSOPHICAL WARRIOR

### 1. "The Deliverer from Error" by Al-Ghazali ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** Authentic autobiography (al-Munqidh min al-Dalāl). Al-Ghazali did leave his prestigious academic post at Nizamiyya of Baghdad at the peak of his career due to epistemological crisis and spiritual doubt.
- **Why description:** "A scholar at the peak of his career demolishes his own framework because truth demanded it. Autobiography as philosophy." — **ACCURATE**

### 2. "The Incoherence of the Incoherence" by Ibn Rushd (Averroes) ✅
- **Status:** REAL  
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** Authentic philosophical work (Tahāfut al-Tahāfut). Written as a direct response to Al-Ghazali's "The Incoherence of the Philosophers."
- **Why description:** "Ibn Rushd's legendary rebuttal to Al-Ghazali — Islamic philosophy's greatest debate on reason vs revelation, fought at the highest intellectual level." — **ACCURATE**

### 3. "The Second Sex" by Simone de Beauvoir ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE  
- **Verification:** Landmark 1949 feminist philosophy text, systematic examination of women's oppression.
- **Why description:** "Courageous, systematic, world-changing. de Beauvoir dismantles centuries of assumptions with surgical precision." — **ACCURATE**

### 4. "Your Silence Will Not Protect You" by Audre Lorde ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 2017 posthumous collection of essays, speeches, and poems. The title quote is from Lorde's famous essay "The Transformation of Silence into Language and Action."
- **Why description:** "Intellectual courage as survival. 'When I dare to be powerful, to use my strength in the service of my vision, then it becomes less important whether I am afraid.'" — **ACCURATE**

### 5. "The Structure of Scientific Revolutions" by Thomas Kuhn ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** Groundbreaking 1962 work that introduced concepts of paradigm shifts in science.
- **Why description:** "How paradigms shift — and why the people inside them can't see the shift coming." — **ACCURATE**

## THE PRACTICAL PHILOSOPHER

### 1. "Permaculture: Principles and Pathways Beyond Sustainability" by David Holmgren ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 2002 foundational permaculture text by co-founder David Holmgren. Explains the 12 permaculture principles from scientific and philosophical perspectives, with emphasis on ethics and energy descent.
- **Why description:** "Design philosophy disguised as agriculture. Ethics embedded in how you arrange a garden." — **ACCURATE**

### 2. "Pedagogy of the Oppressed" by Paulo Freire ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 1968 foundational text in critical pedagogy. Argues education should be liberation, not "banking" of information.
- **Why description:** "Education as liberation. The most practical radical philosophy ever written — still changing classrooms worldwide." — **ACCURATE**

### 3. "Development as Freedom" by Amartya Sen ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 1999 Nobel Prize-winning economist's work on capability approach to development.
- **Why description:** "Economics meets philosophy — freedom isn't just a value, it's the actual mechanism of human development." — **ACCURATE**

### 4. "Hind Swaraj" by Mahatma Gandhi ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 1909 book (also known as "Indian Home Rule") outlining Gandhi's philosophy of swaraj (self-rule) and satyagraha.
- **Why description:** "Self-rule as philosophy. Gandhi's practical ethics — satyagraha as a way of being, not just a tactic." — **ACCURATE**

### 5. "Braiding Sweetgrass" by Robin Wall Kimmerer ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 2013 bestselling work by Potawatomi botanist. Combines indigenous wisdom with botanical science.
- **Why description:** "Indigenous wisdom meets botanical science. Reciprocity with the land as both ancient practice and urgent philosophy." — **ACCURATE**

## THE POETIC EMPIRICIST

### 1. "Landmarks" by Robert Macfarlane ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 2015 book exploring language and landscape in the British archipelago, with glossaries of place-words.
- **Why description:** "Language shapes what we can see in landscape. Macfarlane recovers words for experiences you've had but couldn't name." — **ACCURATE**

### 2. "An Immense World" by Ed Yong ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 2022 bestseller by Pulitzer Prize-winning science journalist. Explores animal perception (Umwelt) and sensory worlds.
- **Why description:** "Animal perception as philosophy — what does the world look like through a bee's compound eye? Changes your sense of reality." — **ACCURATE**

### 3. "The Heart of Haiku" by Jane Hirshfield ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** Short essay/book (Kindle Single, ~29 pages) analyzing Bashō's life and haiku philosophy.
- **Why description:** "Bashō as philosopher. Seventeen syllables can hold more truth than a treatise." — **ACCURATE**

### 4. "Phenomenology of Perception" by Maurice Merleau-Ponty ✅
- **Status:** REAL
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Verification:** 1945 foundational phenomenology text arguing embodied perception shapes reality/consciousness.
- **Why description:** "Your body knows things your mind doesn't. Merleau-Ponty on how perception shapes reality." — **ACCURATE**

### 5. "Braiding Sweetgrass" by Robin Wall Kimmerer ✅
- **Status:** REAL (DUPLICATE)
- **Accuracy:** ACCURATE
- **Why description:** "Learning through attention, reciprocity, and direct experience of the living world." — **ACCURATE**
- **Note:** This book appears TWICE — once in "The Practical Philosopher" and once in "The Poetic Empiricist." Both descriptions are accurate and reflect different aspects of the book. NOT AN ERROR per se, but worth noting.
